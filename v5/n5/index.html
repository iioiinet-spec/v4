General Fluid Dynamics
A fluid is a substance that continually deforms (flows) under an applied shear stress. Unlike solids, fluids (liquids and gases) cannot sustain a static shear force and will move in accordance with the laws of classical physics.

Key Principles:
• Navier-Stokes Equations: The fundamental mathematical framework describing how velocity, pressure, temperature, and density of a moving fluid are interrelated.
• Continuity & Conservation: Mass cannot be created or destroyed within the flow field. This simulation forces an incompressible volume match to generate stable vortices.
• Viscosity & Turbulence: Internal friction determines whether a flow is smooth (laminar) or chaotic (turbulent). Micro-eddies naturally dissolve in thin air.
Simulation Architecture
This system implements a real-time 2D Eulerian fluid grid algorithm natively in JavaScript.

Interactive Controls:
• [Mouse Drag] Inject vector force and dye density into the fixed grid.
• [Z Key] Trigger an explosive paint splash at the current pointer location.
• [Spacebar] Toggle Pause / Resume simulation thread.
• [C Key] Clear all active fields and canvas buffers.

Graphics Calibration:
• Features Linear Interpolation to bridge sampling gaps during high-speed mouse sweeps.
• Utilizes Bilinear Upscaling and glow effects to blur rigid pixel grids into organic smoke structures.
